A software ‚Äòbug‚Äô is an error or flaw in code that causes the program to behave in unintended ways. In office hours, we‚Äôre available to help spot bugs, but there are also ways for you to debug yourself!

  2. Review applicable concepts. Are you working with the modulo (%) operator? Factorials? Functions? You may have forgot a characteristic of the concept, such as that 0! == 1 and function definitions require specific syntax. Brush up on concepts to be sure you understand them!
  3. Take it to paper. If you‚Äôre really stuck, you might want to revisit reworking the steps of your program. Once you have written down pseudocode for the assignment, you can compare it with your code‚Äôs implementation! This is also a great way to start writing a program.
